## 3. FICO Scores: The Mechanics of Credit

The FICO score ranges from 300 to 850:

1.  **Payment History (35%):** Records on-time payments. A single 30-day late payment can drop a high score by 100 points.
2.  **Amounts Owed / Credit Utilization (30%):** The ratio of balances to credit limits. Aim to keep utilization below **10%** (strictly under 30%).
3.  **Length of Credit History (15%):** Age of oldest, newest, and average accounts. Keep old no-fee cards open.
4.  **New Credit (10%):** Hard inquiries and recently opened accounts. Apply for credit sparingly.
5.  **Credit Mix (10%):** Diversity of credit accounts (revolving cards vs. installment loans).

## 4. Major Asset Classes & Guidelines

### A. Houses (Primary Residences)
*   **Amortization Drag:** Early mortgage payments go almost entirely to interest. Equity is built very slowly in the first 7–10 years.
*   **The 28/36 Rule:**
    *   Housing expenses (PITI: Principal, Interest, Taxes, Insurance) should not exceed **28%** of gross monthly income.
    *   Total debt payments (PITI + cars, credit cards, student loans) should not exceed **36%** of gross income (see [[Major Purchases Model]]).
*   **20% Down Payment:** Avoids **PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)**, preserving monthly cash flow.

### B. Vehicles (Cars)
*   **Depreciation Curve:** New vehicles lose ~20% of their value in year one, and ~15% per year for years 2–5. By year 5, a car is worth only **40–50%** of its MSRP.
*   **The 20/4/10 Rule:**
    *   Put at least **20% down**.
    *   Limit the loan term to **4 years**.
    *   Keep total auto costs (payment, gas, insurance, maintenance) under **10%** of gross monthly income.

### C. Rental Properties
*   **Cash Flow Formula:**
    $$\text{Cash Flow} = \text{Gross Rent} - (\text{PITI} + \text{Property Management} + \text{Maintenance Reserve} + \text{Vacancy Reserve})$$
*   **Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate):**
    $$\text{Cap Rate} = \frac{\text{Net Operating Income (NOI)}}{\text{Purchase Price}}$$
*   **DSCR (Debt Service Coverage Ratio):**
    $$\text{DSCR} = \frac{\text{NOI}}{\text{Annual Mortgage Payments}}$$
    *   Lenders typically require a DSCR of **1.20 to 1.25** to approve rental financing.

### D. Small Business Startups
*   **Risk Profile:** ~50% of small businesses fail in the first 5 years, primarily due to cash flow depletion.
*   **Startup Math:**
    *   **Burn Rate:** The rate at which cash reserves are consumed monthly.
    *   **Runway:** How long the company can survive before running out of money.
        $$\text{Runway (Months)} = \frac{\text{Available Cash}}{\text{Monthly Burn Rate}}$$
