# Financial Brain Framework
### The 3x3 Earner-Spender Matrix and Grading Logic

This document details the core classification logic and heuristics for the **Blackbox Financial Brain**—the cognitive engine designed to grade, score, and guide users on cash flow efficiency.

Prioritizing **cash flow preservation** and exposing **debt/payment drag**, the engine coaches users to reclaim their income as their primary wealth-building tool.

---

## 1. Core Premise: The Cash Flow Equation

The foundational metric of Blackbox is **Net Cash Flow (Margin)** rather than gross income or simple static budgets.

$$\text{Net Cash Flow} = \text{Gross Income} - \text{Taxes} - \text{Essential Living Costs} - \text{Debt/Payment Obligations}$$

### The Payment Trap vs. The Livable Base
*   **The Payment Trap:** A household earning **$15,000/month** (High Income) with **$13,500/month** in debt payments, car loans, and mortgage overhead operates on a net cash flow of **$1,500/month**. This household is highly fragile and exposed to massive bankruptcy risk.
*   **The Livable Base:** A household earning **$4,500/month** (Low-Medium Income) with **$3,000/month** in total living costs and **$0** in debt payments operates on a net cash flow of **$1,500/month**. Despite lower gross earnings, this household has identical free cash flow, zero leverage risk, and higher agility.

## 3. The 3x3 Earner-Spender Tiering System

To provide targeted guidance, Blackbox classifies users into a **3x3 Matrix** based on their **Gross Monthly Income (Earner Tier)** and **Outflow Efficiency (Spender Tier)**.

### A. Earner Tiers (Absolute Dollars)
Classified by gross monthly income relative to the national middle 50% benchmarks (see [[Personal Finance Handbook]] for statistics):
1.  **Low Earner (E-Low):** Under **$3,500/month** (<$42k/yr). *Focus: Maximizing income and absolute budget survival.*
2.  **Medium Earner (E-Med):** **$3,500 to $12,500/month** ($42k to $150k/yr). *Focus: Debt elimination and systematic saving.*
3.  **High Earner (E-High):** Over **$12,500/month** (>$150k/yr). *Focus: Wealth building and avoiding lifestyle inflation.*

### B. Spender Tiers (Relative Ratio)
Rather than absolute spending dollars, spenders are classified by their **Outflow-to-Income Ratio**:

$$\text{Outflow Ratio} = \frac{\text{Total Monthly Outflow (Housing + Transport + Debt + Budget Spending)}}{\text{Gross Monthly Income}}$$

1.  **Low Spender (S-Low):** Outflow Ratio **< 55%** of income. (High cash flow margin).
2.  **Medium Spender (S-Med):** Outflow Ratio **55% to 85%** of income. (Typical middle-class posture).
3.  **High Spender (S-High):** Outflow Ratio **> 85%** of income. ( paycheck-to-paycheck; heavily leveraged).

## 5. Implementation Heuristics for the Codebase

These rules drive the grading algorithms in `script.js`:
*   **Earner Tier:** Determined via `getEarnerTier(income)`.
*   **Spender Tier:** Determined via `getSpenderTier(outflow, income)`.
*   **Debt Penalty:** Deduct **10 points** from the [[Health Score]] for every active non-mortgage debt payment (car, college, medical) that exceeds **10%** of gross income.
*   **Car Payment Cap:** Deduct **15 points** if monthly car payments exceed **15%** of monthly income.
*   **Mortgage Cap:** Deduct **15 points** if monthly mortgage + bills exceed **30%** of gross income (see [[Major Purchases Model]]).
*   **Savings Rate Reward:** Add **10 points** if the savings rate is **>20%** of gross income.
